titleEnglish: Shri Vishnu krit Shri Brahma Stava (In Padma Purana)
description: Shri Vishnu-krit Brahma Stava is a hymn from the Padma Purana (Srishti Khanda, Chapter 29) in which Lord Vishnu himself praises Lord Brahma as the boundless, pure-hearted, four-faced creator of the worlds, radiant as a thousand suns. This rare stava, in which the Preserver bows to the Creator, celebrates Brahma as the origin of all sacrifice and creation and prays for purity of heart and release from the cycle of samsara.

significance: <p>This Brahma Stava is preserved in the <strong>Padma Purana (Srishti Khanda, Chapter 29)</strong> and is uniquely the hymn of praise addressed to Lord Brahma by Bhagavan Vishnu himself.</p> <p>Across its verses Vishnu salutes Brahma as the boundless, pure-hearted, thousand-armed creator, radiant as a thousand suns, the four-faced Prapitamaha and the primeval source of the worlds.</p> <p>That the Preserver bows to the Creator makes this stava a rare and exalted testament to Brahma's supremacy as the origin of all yajna and creation.</p>
benefits: <ul><li>Invokes the creative grace of Brahma as praised by Vishnu, the Preserver himself.</li><li>Purifies the heart and cultivates the pure-minded devotion the hymn describes.</li><li>Deepens realization of Brahma as both the vast, visible form and the subtle inner reality.</li><li>Inspires humility, echoing Vishnu's own surrender before the four-faced Lord.</li><li>Draws the aspirant toward release from the round of birth and death (samsara-chakra).</li></ul>
recitationMethod: <p>Recite this stava after bathing, seated in a clean place facing east, having first offered obeisance to both Vishnu and Brahma with a serene and concentrated mind.</p> <p>As a hymn born of deep meditation (samadhi), it is best chanted slowly and contemplatively, dwelling on the wonder of the Preserver praising the Creator.</p>
viniyog: <p>From the Srishti Khanda of the Padma Purana, this is the praise of Brahma spoken by Vishnu; its traditional application is the worship of Brahma the four-faced creator for purity of heart and freedom from samsara.</p>
