description: The Shri Brahma Ashtottara Shatanamavali is a devotional litany of the one hundred and eight names of Lord Brahma, the Creator and Grandsire of the worlds, drawn from the Skanda Purana. Each name is an epithet recalling a form, quality, or holy abode of the four-faced Lord, chanted in worship, meditation, and remembrance.

significance: <p>The Shri Brahma Ashtottar Namavali is a garland of one hundred and eight sacred names of Lord Brahma, the Creator and Grandsire of the worlds. Drawn from the Skanda Purana, each name recalls a form or attribute of the deity as he abides in different holy places, from Pushkar to Prabhasa.</p> <p>Chanting these names one by one, each sealed with the salutation <em>namah</em>, is a complete act of devotion in itself. The practice is meant to fix the mind upon the qualities of the four-faced Lord and to offer him reverence through the power of his own names.</p>
benefits: <ul><li>Draws the mind toward Lord Brahma as Creator, teacher of the Vedas, and grandsire of all beings.</li><li>Serves as a devotional offering (archana) when the names are chanted with flowers or unbroken rice.</li><li>Is traditionally held to cleanse faults of thought, speech, and action when recited or heard with faith.</li><li>Cultivates steadiness, humility, and reverence through repeated remembrance of the sacred names.</li><li>Prepares the heart for deeper worship, mantra japa, or meditation upon the deity.</li></ul>
recitationMethod: <p>Sit in a clean place facing east, having bathed and calmed the mind, ideally in the early morning or at dusk. Recite the names in sequence, beginning with <em>Om Brahmane namah</em>, either simply as remembrance or as an archana while offering flowers and unbroken rice with each name.</p> <p>The whole namavali of one hundred and eight names may be completed in a single sitting. The Kartik Purnima, especially when joined with the Krittika nakshatra, is regarded as an especially auspicious time to praise Lord Brahma by these names.</p>
viniyog: <p>These names are chanted during the archana portion of Brahma worship, when each name is offered together with flowers, rice, or sandal paste at the feet of the deity, and may also be recited alone as a devotional remembrance.</p>
