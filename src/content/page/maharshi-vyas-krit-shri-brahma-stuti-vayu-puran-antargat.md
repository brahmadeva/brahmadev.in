titleDevanagri: महर्षिव्यास कृता श्री ब्रह्मा स्तुतिः (वायुपुराण अंतर्गत)
titleEnglish: Maharshi Vyas krit Shri Brahma Stuti (In Vayu Purana)
description: A hymn to Lord Brahma from the opening chapter of the Vayu Purana, composed by Maharshi Vyasa, who bows to Brahma as the eternal, unconquered creator of the worlds, endowed with the fourfold perfection of knowledge, dispassion, sovereignty, and righteousness. Recited as an invocation before the study of the Puranas, it expresses the sage's reverent surrender to the source of all sacred knowledge.

significance: <p>This Brahma Stuti opens the first chapter of the <em>Vayu Purana</em> and is composed by <strong>Maharshi Vyasa</strong>, the great sage and arranger of the Vedas and Puranas.</p> <p>Vyasa bows to Brahma as the eternal, immutable lord of the three worlds, the omniscient and unconquered creator endowed with the fourfold perfection of knowledge, dispassion, sovereignty, and righteousness. Seeking to hear the rare ancient narratives, the sage takes refuge in the unborn Vishvakarma, witness of all the worlds, before receiving the Purana.</p>
benefits: <ul><li>Invokes Brahma as the wellspring of sacred knowledge, blessing the study and transmission of scripture.</li><li>Inspires the fourfold virtues of wisdom, dispassion, mastery, and dharma that the verses extol.</li><li>Recitation before Puranic study fosters humility and a receptive, reverent mind.</li><li>Aligns the devotee with the example of Vyasa, cultivating surrender to the source of all learning.</li></ul>
recitationMethod: <p>Following Vyasa's example, this stuti is fittingly recited at the outset of studying or reciting the Puranas, as an invocation for grace and clarity.</p> <p>The devotee may recite it seated calmly, with folded hands and an attentive mind, offering salutation to Brahma before taking up sacred texts.</p>
viniyog: <p>The composer (rishi) of this eulogy is <strong>Maharshi Vyasa</strong> and the presiding deity is Lord <strong>Brahma</strong>. It is applied as an invocatory prayer preceding the study and recitation of the Puranas, seeking the creator's grace upon sacred learning.</p>
