titleEnglish: Shri Brahma Kavach (From Mahanirvan Tantra)

description: "The Shri Brahma Kavacham from the Mahanirvana Tantra is a protective hymn (kavach) addressed to the formless, attributeless Supreme Brahman, the indwelling Self of all beings."
sourceName: Mahanirvana Tantra (महानिर्वाण तंत्र)

sourceLink: https://sanskritdocuments.org/doc_deities_misc/brahmakavacham.html

# significance: <p>This Brahma Kavacham is drawn from the Mahanirvana Tantra and is addressed to the formless, attributeless Supreme (Nirguna Parabrahman), the indwelling Self of all. Its nucleus is the seed-mantra Om Sachchidekam Brahma, framed by nyasa, dhyana and the Parabrahma Gayatri.</p> <p>As a kavach it establishes the Supreme Brahman upon the limbs of the aspirant as Paramatma, Parameshvara and Vishvatma, so that the whole body is guarded by eternal, all-pervading consciousness. Its declared aim is that the reciter attains knowledge of Brahman and becomes one with it.</p>
# benefits: "<ul><li>Surrounds the seeker with the protection of the eternal, formless Brahman.</li><li>Supports the fourfold aim of life: dharma, artha, kama and moksha.</li><li>Leads the devotee toward brahma-jnana and identity with the Supreme.</li><li>Steadies the mind through nyasa, dhyana and mantra upon the Self within the heart.</li><li>Grants inner fearlessness and a refuge in the imperishable reality.</li></ul>"

# recitationMethod: <p>Recite the kavach after performing the rishyadi-nyasa, kara-nyasa and hridayadi-nyasa given in the text, followed by the dhyana and the mantra Om Sachchidekam Brahma. It is best undertaken with concentration in a clean, quiet place, seated after purification.</p> <p>Tradition holds that the kavach may be inscribed on birch-bark, enclosed in a golden amulet and worn on the neck or the right arm; a worthy disciple should receive it from the guru with devotion.</p>
# viniyog: <p>Om, of this Sri Parabrahma Kavach the seer (rishi) is Sadashiva, the metre (chhanda) is Anushtup, and the deity (devata) is the attributeless, all-indwelling Supreme Brahman; it is employed for the attainment of the fourfold fruit of life.</p>
