titleDevanagri: श्री गायत्री अष्टोत्तर शतनामावली
titleEnglish: Shri Gayatri Ashtottar Shatnamavali

description: The Shri Gayatri Ashtottar Shatnamavali is a devotional litany of the one hundred and eight names of Goddess Gayatri, the mother of the Vedas and the divine consort of Lord Brahma. Each name extols an aspect of her being, from her radiance like the rising sun to her abiding presence within the orbs of the sun, moon, fire, wind, and sky. Recited as archana during the daily sandhya, it honors her as the living embodiment of the sacred Gayatri mantra and the source of spiritual wisdom.

# sourceLink: https://sanskritdocuments.org/doc_devii/gAyatryaShTottarashatanAmAvaliH.html

significance: <p>The Shri Gayatri Ashtottar Shatnamavali is a litany of one hundred and eight names of Goddess Gayatri, the mother of the Vedas and the divine consort of Lord Brahma. Each name unveils an aspect of her being, from her radiance like the rising sun to her presence within the sun, moon, fire, wind, and sky.</p> <p>To chant these names is to adore the very embodiment of the sacred Gayatri mantra as a living Goddess. The list gathers her forms as Savitri, Saraswati, and the great feminine power that upholds knowledge and creation.</p>

benefits: <ul><li>Invokes Gayatri as the source of the Vedas and the light of spiritual wisdom.</li><li>Nurtures clarity of intellect (dhi) and purity of thought through her sacred names.</li><li>Is offered as devotional archana to the Mother alongside flowers and unbroken rice.</li><li>Fosters inner discipline and reverence suited to those who practice the Gayatri mantra.</li><li>Held to dispel inner darkness and to draw the aspirant toward higher realization.</li></ul>

# recitationMethod: <p>Chant the names at the sacred junctures of the day (sandhya) at dawn, noon, or dusk, after bathing and purifying oneself, seated facing east. Begin with <em>Om Tarunaditya-sankashayai namah</em> and proceed through all one hundred and eight names, each closing with <em>namah</em>.</p> <p>The namavali may be recited on its own or as archana while offering flowers to an image or yantra of the Goddess. Those who regularly practice the Gayatri mantra often add this recitation as an accompanying devotion.</p>

# viniyog: <p>This namavali is applied within the worship of Gayatri and in conjunction with Gayatri japa, where each name may be offered with flowers during archana or recited as loving remembrance of the Mother of the Vedas.</p>
