description: The Hiranyagarbha Suktam (Rigveda 10.121) is a revered Vedic hymn that adores Hiranyagarbha, the golden cosmic source identified with Prajapati and Brahma, who arose in the beginning as the one supreme lord and upholds heaven and earth. Through its recurring refrain, it invokes this creator of all beings, waters and worlds with reverent oblation, offering devotees a profound contemplation of the origin and sustainer of the universe.

significance: <p>The Hiranyagarbha Suktam is a celebrated hymn of the Rigveda (Mandala 10, Sukta 121) that contemplates Hiranyagarbha, the golden cosmic womb or embryo, who arose in the beginning as the one lord of all that came to be. This Hiranyagarbha is identified with Prajapati and Brahma, the creative source who upholds heaven and earth.</p> <p>Each verse marvels at the one who sustains the waters, the mountains, the sky and all creatures, closing with the searching refrain kasmai devaya havisha vidhema, to which god shall we offer our oblation. As a suktam it is a Vedic invocation of the supreme creator through hymn and sacrifice.</p>
benefits: <ul><li>Invokes Hiranyagarbha, the golden source of creation, as the one supreme lord.</li><li>Bestows the merit and sanctity of chanting an ancient Rigvedic hymn.</li><li>Deepens contemplation of the cosmic, all-supporting aspect of Brahma-Prajapati.</li><li>Draws down blessings of wellbeing, sovereignty and abundance sought in its verses.</li><li>Elevates the mind from the created world toward its one imperishable origin.</li></ul>
recitationMethod: <p>Being a Vedic hymn, it is best chanted with attention to correct pronunciation and the traditional accents, preferably learned from a knowledgeable reciter. It is commonly recited during oblation and havan, and may also be taken up in the morning after purification.</p> <p>Devotees offer havis into the sacred fire with each verse, or recite the sukta reverently as a daily contemplation of Prajapati; a settled, meditative attitude enhances its power.</p>
viniyog: <p>Of this Hiranyagarbha Sukta the seer (rishi) is Hiranyagarbha Prajapatya, the metre (chhanda) is Trishtup, and the deity (devata) is Ka, that is Prajapati; it is employed in Vedic worship and oblation to the supreme creator.</p>
