significance: <p>The Shri Saraswati Ashtottarshat Namavali is a sacred one hundred and eight names of Goddess Saraswati, the deity of speech, learning, and the arts, and the divine consort of Lord Brahma. Each name reveals a facet of her grace, from the lotus-seated bearer of the book to the very form of wisdom, the Vedas, and pure sound.</p> <p>Reciting these names is a devotional way to seek the presence of the Goddess who bestows knowledge and eloquence. The litany also honours her as Vagdevi, Bharati, and Brahmajaya, the shining companion of the Creator.</p>

benefits: <ul><li>Invokes Saraswati as the giver of learning, wisdom, and refined speech.</li><li>Supports students, teachers, and artists in their pursuit of knowledge and skill.</li><li>Is offered as archana with white flowers and unbroken rice during her worship.</li><li>Cultivates clarity of expression and a mind inclined toward study and truth.</li><li>Traditionally held to remove ignorance and to bless devotees with discernment.</li></ul>

# recitationMethod: <p>After bathing and settling the mind, sit in a clean place, preferably in the morning, and recite the names beginning with <em>Om Saraswatyai namah</em> through to the one hundred and eighth. The recitation may be simple remembrance or an archana in which each name is offered with white flowers before an image of the Goddess.</p> <p>Vasant Panchami and other days sacred to learning are especially fitting for this recitation, and students may take up the practice before study or examination as an act of devotion.</p>
# viniyog: <p>The namavali is used in the archana of Saraswati, where each name is offered with flowers or rice, and it may also accompany Saraswati mantra practice or be recited alone as a prayer for wisdom and eloquence.</p>
