description: Shri Saraswati Giti is a lyrical Sanskrit devotional song to the Goddess Saraswati (Bharati), deity of speech and learning, drawn from the Kavya Tarangini. Built around a recurring refrain, it is the humble prayer of a devotee who longs for wisdom and eloquent speech, entreating the Goddess who dwells upon the white lotus to abide upon his tongue and grant him a place as her servant.

significance: <p>The Shri Saraswati Giti is a lyrical devotional song addressed to Bharati, the Goddess Saraswati, drawn from the Sanskrit <em>Kavya Tarangini</em>. Composed around a recurring refrain (<em>dhruvam</em>), it is set in the tender, melodic manner of a <em>giti</em>, in which the devotee prays to abide as a humble servant at the feet of the Goddess.</p> <p>In sweet and graceful Sanskrit, the singer laments the barrenness of a life bereft of learning and entreats the Goddess — she who dwells upon the white lotus and removes the sins of the world — to place the ceaseless song of wisdom upon the tongue.</p>
benefits: <ul><li>Awakens love of learning and the grace of eloquent, melodious speech.</li><li>Nurtures devotion to the Goddess of wisdom through the medium of song.</li><li>Removes the dullness of an untutored mind and kindles the light of knowledge.</li><li>Inspires the arts, music and poetic expression.</li><li>Fosters humility and a heart devoted to the service of the Divine Mother.</li></ul>
recitationMethod: <p>As a <em>giti</em>, this hymn is best sung with devotion and a melodious voice, returning to the refrain (<em>dhruvam</em>) after each verse. It may be offered in the morning or during personal worship, before an image of the Goddess Saraswati.</p> <p>Sing it slowly and with feeling, allowing its meaning to steep the heart in devotion. It is especially fitting during music practice, study and on Vasant Panchami.</p>
viniyog: <p>The <em>Devata</em> of this song is Saraswati (Bharati), the Goddess of speech and learning. Being a devotional <em>giti</em> rather than a Vedic mantra, it carries no formal viniyog; it is offered simply for the awakening of wisdom, eloquence and devotion.</p>
