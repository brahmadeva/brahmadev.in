titleDevanagri: श्री गायत्री स्तोत्रम् (देवी भागवत पुराण अंतर्गत)
titleEnglish: Shri Gayatri Stotram (From Devi Bhagvata Purana)
description: The Shri Gayatri Stotram from the Devi Bhagavata Puranais a hymn imparted by Bhagavan Narayana to the sage Narada in praise of the Goddess Sandhya-Gayatri, the primordial Adi Shakti and Mother of the worlds. It extols her as the power pervading all creation - the sacred rivers, the subtle channels of the body, and every plane of existence - and promises purification, spiritual attainment and liberation to those who recite it at the twilight worship.

sourceName: Devi Bhagvata Purana, Skandha 12, Adhyay 5 (देवीभागवतपुराणम्/स्कन्धः १२/अध्यायः ०५)
sourceLink: https://sa.wikisource.org/s/n8u

significance: <p>This Gayatri Stotram appears in the Devi Bhagavata Purana (Skandha 12, Adhyaya 5), where it is imparted by Bhagavan Narayana to the sage Narada. It extols the Goddess Sandhya-Gayatri as the primordial <em>Adi Shakti</em>, the mother of the worlds and the bestower of grace upon her devotees, who pervades all of creation.</p> <p>The hymn celebrates her threefold form - a child at dawn, youthful at midday and aged at dusk - and identifies her with the great sacred rivers, the subtle channels (<em>nadis</em>) of the body, and the animating power present upon every plane of existence. It is a profound meditation upon the Goddess as the sacred twilight worship itself, personified.</p>

# benefits: <ul><li>Purifies the practitioner and is extolled as a great remover of sins when recited at the <em>sandhya</em> hours.</li><li>Awakens spiritual illumination and reveals the all-pervading presence of the Divine Mother.</li><li>Strengthens the daily discipline of <em>sandhya-vandana</em> and Gayatri upasana.</li><li>Bestows inner peace, devotion and the fruit of pilgrimage, austerity and worship.</li><li>Guides the aspirant from worldly wellbeing toward final liberation.</li></ul>
# recitationMethod: <p>As the stotram itself directs, recite it with a composed and gathered mind during the <em>sandhya</em> hours - at dawn, midday and dusk - after performing the twilight worship. It may also be recited at the time of ritual bathing, contemplating the Goddess within the flowing waters.</p> <p>Read it with devotion and steady attention, ideally each day. Even reverent listening to this hymn is traditionally held to be purifying.</p>
# viniyog: <p>The <em>Devata</em> of this hymn is Sandhya-Gayatri, the <em>Adi Shakti</em>. In keeping with the Gayatri tradition the associated <em>Chhanda</em> is Gayatri; the recitation is offered for purification, illumination and liberation during the sacred twilight worship.</p>
