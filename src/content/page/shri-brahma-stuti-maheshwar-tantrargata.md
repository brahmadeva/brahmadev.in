titleDevanagri: श्री ब्रह्मा स्तुतिः (श्री माहेश्वरतन्त्रान्तर्गता)
titleEnglish: Shri Brahma Stuti (From Maheshwar Tantra)

description: The Shri Brahma Stuti from the third chapter of the Maheshwar Tantra is a hymn in which the gods adore Lord Brahma as the sole creator, sustainer, and dissolver of the world, the all-witnessing inner soul of the cosmos, and Hiranyagarbha dwelling within the egg formed of the twenty-four principles. Alarmed by a sudden, all-consuming fire that terrifies gods, demons, and mortals alike, they beseech the wise Grandsire, than whom nothing is higher, to discern its cause and ordain its remedy.

significance: <p>This Brahma Stuti is drawn from the third chapter (Tritiya Patala) of the <em>Maheshwar Tantra</em> and is spoken by the gods in adoration of Lord Brahma.</p> <p>It salutes him as the sole creator, sustainer, and dissolver of the world, the witness of all, the inner soul of the cosmos, and <strong>Hiranyagarbha</strong> dwelling within the egg formed of the twenty-four principles. Alarmed by a sudden all-consuming fire that threatens gods, demons, and mortals alike, the devas beseech the wise Grandsire, than whom nothing is higher, to discern its cause and ordain its remedy.</p>

benefits: <ul><li>Adores Brahma as Hiranyagarbha and the innermost soul of the universe, elevating devotional contemplation.</li><li>Invokes the creator as protector when unforeseen calamity and fear threaten the world.</li><li>Recitation fosters trust that the wise Grandsire perceives the cause of distress and ordains its relief.</li><li>Reinforces recognition of Brahma as the supreme reality beyond whom nothing is greater.</li></ul>
# recitationMethod: <p>This stuti may be recited with reverence during worship of Brahma, or when seeking protection and clarity amid sudden difficulty or danger.</p> <p>Seated in a pure and quiet place, the devotee may recite it attentively, meditating on Brahma as Hiranyagarbha and the sole refuge of the worlds.</p>
# viniyog: <p>The reciters of this eulogy are the <strong>devas</strong> and the presiding deity is Lord <strong>Brahma</strong>, adored as Hiranyagarbha. Preserved within the Maheshwar Tantra, it is applied as a tantric hymn of adoration and appeal for the creator's protective grace.</p>
