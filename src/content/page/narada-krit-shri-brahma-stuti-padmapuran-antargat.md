titleDevanagri: नारद कृता श्री ब्रह्मा स्तुतिः (पद्मपुराण अंतर्गत)
titleEnglish: Narada Krit Shri Brahma Stuti (From Padma Purana)

description: The Narada krit Shri Brahma Stuti, from the Srishti Khanda of the Padma Purana, is a hymn in which the celestial sage Narada adores Lord Brahma as the thousand-headed cosmic Purusha, the source of the Vedas, sacrifice, the fourfold social order, and every being. Echoing the Purusha Sukta and the images of the banyan within its seed and the plantain and its leaves, it proclaims that the whole universe abides within its creator.


significance: <p>This eulogy is composed by the celestial sage <strong>Narada</strong> and appears in the Srishti Khanda (the section on creation) of the <em>Padma Purana</em>.</p> <p>Echoing the imagery of the Purusha Sukta, it adores Brahma as the thousand-headed cosmic Purusha from whom sacrifice, the Vedas, the fourfold social order, the elements, and all beings arise. Through the analogies of the banyan within its seed and the plantain and its leaves, Narada proclaims that the whole universe abides within the creator, and rejoices that his long penance has borne fruit in the vision of the Lord.</p>

benefits: <ul><li>Reveals Brahma as the all-pervading Purusha, source of the Vedas, sacrifice, and every being.</li><li>Deepens the understanding that all creation abides within the creator, fostering devotion and awe.</li><li>Following Narada's example, it kindles the fruit of sincere penance and the longing for divine vision (darshan).</li><li>Its imagery aids contemplation of the one reality underlying the manifold world.</li></ul>

# recitationMethod: <p>This stuti may be recited with devotion after worship of Brahma, dwelling on each verse that unfolds the creator as the source of all existence.</p> <p>It is well suited to recitation during periods of intensified sadhana or penance, in the trusting spirit with which Narada sought and received the Lord's grace.</p>

# viniyog: <p>The composer (rishi) of this hymn is the divine sage <strong>Narada</strong> and the presiding deity is Lord <strong>Brahma</strong>, extolled as the cosmic Purusha. It is applied as a devotional adoration of the creator, offered in surrender and in the pursuit of his vision.</p>
