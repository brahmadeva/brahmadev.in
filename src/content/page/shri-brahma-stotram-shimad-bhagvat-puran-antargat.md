titleEnglish: Shri Brahma Stotram (From Bhagvata Purana)

description: The Shri Brahma Stotram, from the Seventh Skandha of the Shrimad Bhagavata Purana, is a hymn uttered by Hiranyakashipu in praise of Lord Brahma. It extols Brahma as the primal seed of the universe, the self-luminous Hiranyagarbha, and the inner Self of all beings, who through the three gunas creates, sustains, and dissolves the cosmos.

sourceName: "Shimad Bhagvat Purana, Skanda 7, Adhyay 3 (श्रीमद्भागवतपुराणम्/स्कन्धः ७/अध्यायः ३) "
sourceLink: https://sa.wikisource.org/s/bn4
featured: true

significance: <p>This Brahma Stotram is drawn from the <strong>Shrimad Bhagavata Mahapurana, Seventh Skandha, Third Chapter</strong>, where it is uttered by Hiranyakashipu at the culmination of his severe penance to propitiate Lord Brahma.</p> <p>The hymn praises Brahma as the primal seed of the universe, the embodiment of knowledge and realization, the threefold power that creates, protects and dissolves, and the inner Self of all living beings.</p> <p>It stands as a striking testimony that even a great asura recognised Brahma as Hiranyagarbha, the self-luminous Lord who pervades all that moves and does not move.</p>

# benefits: <ul><li>Invokes the creative grace of Brahma, the primal cause and seed of the cosmos.</li><li>Strengthens focus and resolve, being the fruit of intense, one-pointed penance.</li><li>Deepens understanding of Brahma as the source of knowledge and realization.</li><li>Cultivates awareness of the Lord as the indwelling Self and giver of life.</li><li>Fosters reverence for the threefold divine power of creation, sustenance and dissolution.</li></ul>

# recitationMethod: <p>Recite this stotram in the early morning hours after purification, seated facing east or north with a settled and one-pointed mind.</p> <p>Since the hymn arose from sustained tapas, it is best chanted with steady concentration and sincere aspiration rather than haste, as part of one's regular devotional practice.</p>
# viniyog: <p>From the Seventh Skandha of the Shrimad Bhagavata Purana, this is Hiranyakashipu's praise of Brahma; its ritual application is the devout worship of Brahma as the primal creator and inner Self.</p>
