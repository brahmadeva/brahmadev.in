titleDevanagri: देवकृत ब्रह्म स्तोत्रम् (स्कंद पुराण अंतर्गत)
titleEnglish: Devakrit Brahma Storam (From Skanda Purana)

description: The Brahma Stotram, from the Suta Samhita of the Skanda Purana, is a hymn of praise offered by the gods to Lord Brahma. the Devas salute Brahma as Hiranyagarbha, Prajapati, and Pitamaha, the four-faced creator and self-luminous witness who bestows the knowledge of the Absolute and carries devotees across the ocean of worldly existence.

sourceName: Skanda Purana (स्कंद पुराण)


significance: <p>This Deva-krit Brahma Stotram appears in the <strong>Suta Samhita of the Skanda Purana</strong> and is the hymn offered by the gods (Devas) in praise of Lord Brahma.</p> <p>Across its twenty verses the Devas salute Brahma through a rich garland of epithets, hailing him as Hiranyagarbha, Prajapati, Pitamaha, the four-faced creator, and the self-luminous witness of all states of being.</p> <p>The hymn extols Brahma both as the giver of Brahma-vidya, the knowledge of the Absolute, and as the self-effulgent Truth beyond all attributes.</p>

benefits: <ul><li>Awakens the higher knowledge that carries one across the ocean of worldly existence.</li><li>Confers wisdom, discernment and clarity of intellect upon the reciter.</li><li>Removes sorrow and the pairs of opposites (dvandva) that afflict the mind.</li><li>Deepens reverence for Brahma as creator, sustainer and witness of all.</li><li>Cultivates purity and equanimity through remembrance of the self-luminous Self.</li></ul>
