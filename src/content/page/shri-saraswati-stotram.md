titleDevanagri: श्री सरस्वती स्तोत्र
titleEnglish: Shri Saraswati Storam

description: The Shri Saraswati Stotram is a devotional hymn traditionally attributed to Lord Brahma in praise of his consort Saraswati, the goddess of speech, wisdom and the arts, envisioned seated upon a white lotus and bearing the vina, the sacred book and the crystal rosary. Woven with her seed-syllables (bija-mantras), it beseeches her to dispel the darkness of ignorance and dullness and to bestow eloquence, discernment and mastery of the scriptures upon the devotee.


significance: <p>The Shri Saraswati Stotram is a devotional hymn traditionally attributed to Lord Brahma himself, who extols his consort Saraswati, the Goddess of speech, wisdom and the arts. She is beheld seated upon a white lotus and swan, bearing the <em>vina</em>, the sacred book and the crystal rosary - the very embodiment of pure, radiant knowledge.</p> <p>Woven through with the seed-syllables (<em>bija-mantras</em>) of the Goddess, the stotram beseeches her to dispel the darkness of ignorance and the dullness of intellect, and to bestow eloquence, discernment and mastery of the scriptures upon the devotee.</p>

benefits: <ul><li>Bestows wisdom, a sharp intellect and clarity of understanding.</li><li>Dispels mental dullness and the darkness of ignorance.</li><li>Grants eloquence and mastery of speech, poetry and the fine arts.</li><li>Aids students and seekers in the study and retention of scripture and learning.</li><li>Cultivates devotion and, through her grace, success in scholarly and creative endeavours.</li></ul>

# recitationMethod: <p>Recite the stotram in the early morning after bathing, seated before an image of the Goddess with a humble and devout heart. The text itself commends recitation on the thirteenth lunar day (<em>Trayodashi</em>) by one who observes celibacy, silence and a simple, sattvic diet.</p> <p>Students and seekers of knowledge may recite it daily; steady, uninterrupted practice across the fortnights is especially praised. It is particularly auspicious on Vasant Panchami and while engaged in the study of the scriptures.</p>
# viniyog: <p>As declared in the hymn, the <em>Rishi</em> is Brahma, the <em>Chhanda</em> is Gayatri and the <em>Devata</em> is Shri Saraswati. The recitation (<em>japa</em>) is undertaken for the fourfold aims of dharma, artha, kama and moksha.</p>
