titleDevanagri: देवकृत श्री ब्रह्मस्तुतिः (मत्स्य पुराण अंतर्गत)
titleEnglish: Devakrit Shri Brahma Stuti (From Matsya Purana)

description: The Deva-krita Brahma Stuti from the Matsya Purana is a philosophical hymn in which the gods praise Lord Brahma as Omkara and the cosmic Purusha whose very body is the universe. Drawing on Samkhya conceptions of the subtle principles and the measure of time, it adores him as the unfathomable cause of creation, sustenance, and dissolution, and as the sure refuge and protector of his devotees.

sourceName: Matsya Purana, Chapter 154, Shlok 7-15 <br/>(मत्स्य पुराण / अध्याय १५४ / श्लोक ७-१५)

sourceLink: https://sa.wikisource.org/s/48n

significance: <p>Found in the one hundred fifty-fourth chapter of the <em>Matsya Purana</em> (verses seven to fifteen), this eulogy is uttered by the gods in profound philosophical praise of Lord Brahma.</p> <p>The hymn identifies Brahma with the primordial <strong>Omkara</strong> and the cosmic Purusha whose body is the universe, the heavens his head and the sun and moon his eyes. Drawing upon Samkhya conceptions of the subtle principles and the measure of time, it hails him as the unfathomable cause of manifestation, sustenance, and dissolution, and the sure refuge of the devoted.</p>

benefits: <ul><li>Reveals Brahma as the cosmic Purusha, deepening contemplation of the divine as the substance of all creation.</li><li>Aids the seeker in perceiving the unity behind the diversity of the manifest world.</li><li>Its philosophical depth supports reflection alongside Samkhya and Vedantic study.</li><li>Recitation nurtures surrender to Brahma as protector, guardian, and endless refuge of his devotees.</li></ul>

recitationMethod: <p>Given its contemplative and metaphysical character, this stuti is best recited slowly and with reflection upon each image of the creator's cosmic form.</p> <p>It suits recitation during study of the Puranas or after formal worship of Brahma, in a quiet and composed state of mind conducive to meditation.</p>
