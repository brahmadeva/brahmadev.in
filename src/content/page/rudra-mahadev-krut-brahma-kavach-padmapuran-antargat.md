titleDevanagri: रुद्र (महादेव) कृत श्री ब्रह्मा कवचम् (पद्मपुराण अंतर्गत)
titleEnglish: Rudra (Mahadev) krit Shri Brahma Kavacham (From Padma Purana)

description: The Rudra-krit Brahma Kavacham, preserved in the Srishti Khanda of the Padma Purana, is a protective hymn in which Lord Shiva (Rudra), following Vishnu, bows before the lotus-born creator Brahma and hymns his sacred names. As an armour of prayer, it invokes Brahma's many names to guard the devotee limb by limb, seeking refuge at the feet of the primordial creator and release from the bondage of worldly existence.


sourceName: Padma Purna, Srushti Khanda, Adhyay 34, Shlok 117-126 <br>(पद्मपुराणम् / खण्डः १ (सृष्टिखण्डम्) / अध्यायः ३४ / श्लोक ११७ - १२६ )
sourceLink: https://sa.wikisource.org/s/i3g
featured: true

significance: <p>This is the Brahma Kavacham revealed by Rudra (Mahadev), preserved in the Srishti Khanda of the Padma Purana, in which Lord Shiva, following Vishnu, bows before the lotus-born creator Brahma and hymns him as Pitamaha, Kamalasana and Paramatma.</p> <p>Being a kavach, it is a protective armour of prayer in which the many sacred names of Brahma are invoked to guard each limb of the devotee, from the feet to the crown of the head. Its spiritual purpose is deliverance from the bondage of worldly existence and refuge at the feet of the primordial creator.</p>

benefits: <ul><li>Envelops the devotee in the protective grace of Brahma, the grandsire of all beings.</li><li>Invokes divine names to shield every part of the body, limb by limb, from harm.</li><li>Helps loosen the bonds of samsara and turns the mind toward liberation.</li><li>Cultivates humility and surrender, following the example of Rudra bowing to the creator.</li><li>Bestows steadiness, inner strength and a sure refuge in times of fear.</li></ul>

recitationMethod: <p>Recite the kavach with devotion after bathing, seated facing east before an image or thought-form of Lord Brahma, ideally at dawn. Read the verses attentively, dwelling on the placement of each divine name upon its corresponding limb as taught in the text.</p> <p>Regular daily recitation, or reading it as part of Brahma worship, deepens its protective effect; conclude with salutations to the creator, as Rudra himself does.</p>

# viniyog: <p>As spoken in the Padma Purana, this kavach is uttered by Rudra in praise of Brahma; the presiding deity (devata) is Lord Brahma, the lotus-born creator, and it is recited for the protection of the body and release from worldly bondage.</p>
